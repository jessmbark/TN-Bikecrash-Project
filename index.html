A Rocky Road

An analysis of Tennessee's bicycle crash data since 2010

By: Jessica M. Barker

When I was a kid, I loved to ride my bike. But as an adult, I don't always feel safe riding one, especially in my home state of Tennessee. I found out that the Tennessee Highway Patrol and the Tennessee Department of Safety & Homeland Security collect annual data on the number of bicycle crashes for each of the state's 95 counties.

The agencies broke crashes down into three categories: fatal, PDO, and injury. PDO crashes refer to crashes that caused more than $400 in damages. The data also "[e]xcludes parking lot and private property crashes as well as crashes with less than $400 damage. Fatal crash numbers may not match NHTSA [National Highway Traffic Safety Administration] crash numbers."

The chart above represents over 5,700 crashes. For every year between 2010 and 2023, injury crashes made up between 80 and 92 percent of all crashes.

The agencies also collected data for the first six months of 2024, which is shown below. Each square represents one crash. Red squares represent fatal crashes, orange squares represent injury crashes, and yellow squares represent crashes that caused over $400 in property damage.

Since 2010, 117 people died in bicycle crashes.

An average year:

  • Sees over 405 total crashes
  • And sees 8 deaths from bicycle crashes

Follow-up work could explore why the state's data may not match national data. Follow-up work could also seek to find records of crashes that resulted in less than $400 in damages.
